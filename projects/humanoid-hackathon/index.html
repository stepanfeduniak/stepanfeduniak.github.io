Humanoid Manipulation Hackathon (Winner)

Sept 2025 Siemens Challenge Winner, Munich

Participated in the Humanoid Manipulation Hackathon in Munich as a one-person team and won the Siemens Challenge.

The Challenge

The task involved picking up randomly scattered electric fuses from a table and precisely mounting them onto a DIN rail. This required high precision and robustness to varying initial conditions.

My Solution: BESO x LeRobot

I implemented BESO (Behavior Evaluation and Synthesis via Optimal Control), a simple yet powerful baseline policy, and adapted it for the LeRobot framework.

  • Explainability: Used XAI techniques to ensure the policy was effectively using all sensor modalities (visual, language, etc.) rather than just memorizing a single trajectory.
  • Robustness: The final policy could instantly recover from failures and continue working in a closed-loop fashion.
